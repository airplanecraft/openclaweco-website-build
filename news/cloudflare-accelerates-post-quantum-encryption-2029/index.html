Cloudflare Accelerates Post-Quantum Encryption Rollout to 2029 Amidst Emerging Quantum Threat

April 8, 2026 · News
post-quantum cryptographyquantum computingcybersecurityencryptioncloudflare

Cloudflare, a leading provider of internet security and CDN services, has announced an accelerated roadmap to achieve full implementation of post-quantum cryptography (PQC) by 2029. This expedited timeline is a direct response to recent research indicating that the qubit scale required to break existing, widely used encryption algorithms is significantly lower than previously anticipated, highlighting an urgent and evolving threat landscape.

The urgency of this shift is further underscored by experts in the field. The CTO of IBM Quantum Safe has warned that quantum computers could potentially execute "moonshot attacks" – large-scale, disruptive decryption operations against high-value targets – as early as 2029. This projection emphasizes the critical need for proactive measures to secure digital infrastructure against future quantum threats.

Cloudflare has a track record of enhancing internet security; it began offering free universal SSL certificates in 2014, a move that significantly boosted web encryption. Its journey toward post-quantum readiness started in 2019 with initial migration preparations. By 2022, Cloudflare had enabled post-quantum encryption for all websites and APIs served through its platform. Currently, over 65% of Cloudflare's user traffic is already protected with post-quantum encryption, establishing a robust defense against impending quantum decryption capabilities.
