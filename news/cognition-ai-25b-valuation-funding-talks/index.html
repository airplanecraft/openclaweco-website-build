AI Coding Firm Cognition Reportedly Seeking Hundreds of Millions, Valuing Company at $25B

April 24, 2026 · News
ai agentdevinai codingstartup valuationautonomous coding

AI coding firm Cognition is reportedly in early discussions to secure hundreds of millions of dollars in a new funding round that could push its valuation to an astonishing $25 billion, according to sources familiar with the matter cited by Bloomberg. This prospective valuation represents a substantial increase from the $10.2 billion figure the company announced in September 2025.

Should the funding talks materialize, it would mark a significant milestone for Cognition, a key player in the burgeoning AI agent and autonomous programming space. The company gained prominence with the introduction of Devin, heralded as the world's first AI software engineer capable of autonomously planning and executing complex engineering tasks, from writing and debugging code to deploying full applications.

The potential $25 billion valuation not only underscores investor confidence in Cognition's groundbreaking technology but also reflects a broader market enthusiasm for AI-driven software development tools and the expanding AI agent ecosystem. In an era of rapid AI advancements, autonomous coding solutions are increasingly viewed as critical for enhancing development efficiency and fostering innovation, attracting considerable capital interest.
