AI's Economic Shift: Human Connection and Relational Work Drive Value in a Post-Commodity Era

April 27, 2026 · News
ai economyautomationvalue shiftrelational workpost-commodity

A burgeoning economic argument posits that Artificial Intelligence (AI) will not simply decimate existing jobs but will fundamentally shift economic value towards sectors where human attributes such as presence, provenance, care, taste, and relationship hold paramount importance.

This perspective, advanced by Alex Imas, introduces the concept of a "post-commodity economy." In this future economic landscape, as the supply of goods and services becomes increasingly abundant and accessible due to widespread automation, their marginal value may diminish. Consequently, the economic premium will pivot to human-centric contributions that are inherently difficult for machines to replicate—those demanding genuine human connection, empathy, and bespoke experiences.

Imas elaborates that rather than rendering human labor obsolete, automation is poised to significantly elevate the value of "relational work." This category encompasses roles deeply rooted in human interaction, trust-building, and emotional intelligence, such as specialized consulting, personalized care, creative arts, bespoke education, and high-touch service industries. In an AI-pervasive future, human capacity for empathy, creativity, and delivering authentic personalized experiences will emerge as critical drivers of economic prosperity.

The prevailing discourse on AI's impact on employment often fixates on job displacement, overlooking a crucial dimension: what new forms of demand will be unlocked when AI-driven supply becomes virtually limitless? Understanding this emergent demand is key to grasping where economic vitality will truly flourish in the age of AI.
