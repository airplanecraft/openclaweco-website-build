Google Forms Elite Team for Coding Models; Sergey Brin Urges DeepMind to Aggressively Pursue AI Agents

April 20, 2026 · News
googledeepmindai agentscoding modelssergey brin

According to sources cited by The Information, tech giant Google is undertaking significant internal initiatives to strengthen its position in the artificial intelligence landscape. A key development is the formation of a specialized "strike team" whose primary objective is to accelerate and enhance Google's coding models. This move signifies Google's substantial investment in improving AI capabilities for code generation, understanding, and development assistance, which holds profound implications for future software development efficiency and quality.

Concurrently, Google co-founder Sergey Brin has reportedly conveyed urgent and direct instructions to staff at its AI laboratory, DeepMind. Brin emphasized that DeepMind must "aggressively pivot" its strategic focus to rapidly catch up and potentially surpass competitors in the highly competitive field of AI agents. He highlighted AI agents as a critical future direction for artificial intelligence, capable of independently executing complex tasks, understanding user intent, and interacting with environments. Brin's direct involvement underscores the urgency and strategic importance Google's leadership places on the AI agent domain, anticipating rapid breakthroughs from DeepMind to reshape the market.

OpenMythos

OpenMythos is an open-source, theoretical implementation of the Anthropic Claude Mythos model, constructed by kyegomez based on publicly available research and speculation. It employs a Recurrent-Depth Transformer (RDT) architecture with three stages: a Prelude, a looped Recurrent Block, and a Coda. The core Recurrent Block allows for multiple iterations to achieve deep reasoning, enabling compute-adaptive and depth-variable reasoning. It supports switchable attention mechanisms (MLA/GQA) and utilizes a sparse Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) with routed and shared experts. OpenMythos aims to deliver performance comparable to models with higher parameter counts through greater parameter efficiency.
